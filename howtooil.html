How to Change Your Own Oil

Supplies Needed

Here are the things needed to change your oil

  • More Oil - You can find the type of oil and how much by clicking Here
  • Another Filter - You can find the kind of oil filter you need Here
  • Rachet and Socket or wrench - You can find a rachet and socket set at a local Walmart or you can get one Here
  • Funnel - Makes it alot easier to pour your new oil into your motor. You can find one at a local store or Here
  • Drain Pan - You need something to put your old oil in. You can get one of these at a local auto parts store or Here

Getting Started

Don't be worried, this is a pretty simple task. Just make sure you follow all of these steps and you'll be good to go!

  • First, you want to make sure your car is cool. If you just drove it, wait awhile for it to cool off or the oil/oil filter may burn you.
  • Second, you want to locate your oil pan and the drain plug sticking out. The oil pan should be directly underneath the motor and the drain plug will be the big bolt on your oil pan. They should look like this.
  • Third, place your drain pan under the drain plug. Find the correct socket size fo rthe drain plug (should be the one that fits tightly and goes all the way over the drain plug).
  • Fourth, attach your socket to your rachet and set it to loosen (grab the socket with one hand and turn the ratchet with the other, it the socet turns left, you're good to go)
  • Fifth, loosen your drain plug off your oil pan. Make sure the oil goes into the drain pan.

Finishing up

  • Next, you want to want to take off your old filter, you'll be able to turn it off with your hand. Go ahead and put your new filter on, just line it up and screw it on.
  • After your oil is done draining, go ahead and put your drain plug back in.
  • Grab your funnel and new oil. There will be a cap you need to screw off (refer to image below). Put your funnel in there and fill it up! Make sure to put your cap back on when you are done.
  • Finally, start your car and check for any leaks around your drain plug and oil filter.

Congrats! You've successfully changed your oil!
